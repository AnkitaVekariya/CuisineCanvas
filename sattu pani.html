Sattu Pani

Sattu Pani is a traditional North Indian drink made from roasted gram flour (sattu). It's incredibly refreshing and nutritious, especially during hot summer days. Here's a simple recipe to make Sattu Pani at home:

Ingridents:

  • 4 tablespoons sattu (roasted gram flour)
  • 3-4 cups chilled water
  • 1-2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 small piece of ginger, grated or finely chopped
  • 1-2 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander leaves Black salt (kala namak) to taste
  • Roasted cumin powder (bhuna jeera powder) to taste Ice cubes (optional)
  • Mint leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

1.

In a mixing bowl, add sattu, chopped green chilies (if using), grated ginger, and chopped coriander leaves. Seasoning:

2.

Add black salt and roasted cumin powder according to your taste preferences. These spices give the drink its characteristic flavor. You can adjust the amount of spices based on your taste preferences.

3.

Slowly pour chilled water into the bowl containing the sattu mixture while continuously stirring to avoid lumps. Mix well until the sattu paste combines smoothly with the water.

4.

Squeeze fresh lemon juice into the mixture. Adjust the amount of lemon juice based on your taste preference for tartness.

5.

Taste the Sattu Pani and adjust the seasoning if needed. Add more black salt or lemon juice if required. You can also add ice cubes to make it more refreshing, although traditionally it's served chilled without ice

6.

Serve Sattu Pani chilled as a refreshing drink on hot summer days. It pairs well with snacks like pakoras, samosas, or any spicy Indian street food. ou can also customize this recipe by adding other ingredients like chopped onions, tomatoes, or even a hint of jaggery for sweetness, according to your taste preference.
