Jalebi

Jalebi is a popular Indian sweet made from fermented batter deep-fried into a pretzel-like shape and then soaked in sugar syrup. Here's a recipe to make jalebi at home:

Ingridents:

  • For the batter:
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 tablespoon chickpea flour (besan)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt (curd)
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder (for color) Water, as needed Ghee or oil, for frying
  • For the sugar syrup:
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Saffron strands (optional)
  • Lemon juice, a few drops

Instructions:

1.

In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, chickpea flour, yogurt, baking soda, and turmeric powder. Gradually add water and mix well to form a smooth, lump-free batter. The consistency should be thick yet pourable. Set the batter aside to ferment for at least 8 hours or overnight.

2.

In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Heat over medium heat until the sugar dissolves completely. Add a few drops of lemon juice to prevent crystallization.

3.

Stir in cardamom powder and saffron strands (if using). Simmer the syrup for 5-7 minutes until it reaches a one-string consistency. Turn off the heat and keep the syrup warm.

4.

Heat ghee or oil in a deep frying pan or kadai over medium heat. Fill a piping bag fitted with a small round nozzle (or use a squeeze bottle) with the fermented batter.

5.

Once the oil is hot, pipe the batter into the hot oil in a spiral or pretzel-like shape. You can also make small circles or swirls. Fry the jalebis until they are golden brown and crispy on both sides, turning them once or twice. Fry them in batches, if necessary.

6.

Once fried, remove the jalebis from the oil using a slotted spoon and immediately dip them into the warm sugar syrup. Let them soak for about 30 seconds to 1 minute. Remove the soaked jalebis from the syrup and transfer them to a plate or serving tray. Repeat the frying and soaking process with the remaining batter.

Serve the jalebis warm or at room temperature. Enjoy the crispy, sweet, and aromatic jalebis as a delicious dessert or snack!
