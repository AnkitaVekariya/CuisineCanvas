palak paneer

Palak Paneer is a popular North Indian dish made with spinach (palak) and Indian cottage cheese (paneer) cooked in a flavorful gravy. Here's a delicious recipe to make it at home:

Ingridents:

  • 250 grams paneer (Indian cottage cheese), cubed
  • 500 grams fresh spinach leaves (palak), washed and chopped
  • 2 medium-sized onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin see
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream or cashew cream (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste,Water, as needed,Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Instructions:

1.

Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add chopped spinach leaves to the boiling water and blanch them for 2-3 minutes.

2.

Drain the spinach and immediately transfer it to a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking process.

3.

Drain again and then blend the blanched spinach into a smooth puree. Set aside.

4.

Heat a little oil in a pan and lightly fry the paneer cubes until they turn golden brown. Remove them from the pan and set aside.

4.

Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw aroma disappears. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy and oil starts separating from the masala.

Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice
