Veg Korma

Veg Korma is a flavorful and creamy Indian dish made with mixed vegetables cooked in a rich and aromatic gravy. Here's a recipe to make Veg Korma at home:

Ingridents:

  • 1 large onion, roughly chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • 1-inch ginger, chopped
  • 4-5 garlic clove
  • 1/4 cup cashews or almonds
  • 2 tablespoons desiccated coconut (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon poppy seeds (khus khus), soaked in warm water for 20 minutes (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, cauliflower), diced
  • 1 potato, diced
  • 1/2 cup paneer (Indian cottage cheese), cubed (optional)
  • 1/4 cup fresh cream or coconut cream
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Instructions:

1.

In a blender, combine chopped onion, tomatoes, ginger, garlic, cashews or almonds, desiccated coconut, yogurt, green chilies, soaked poppy seeds, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Blend everything together until you get a smooth paste. If needed, you can add a little water to help with blending.

2.

Heat oil or ghee in a large pan or kadhai over medium heat. Add bay leaf, cloves, cardamom pods, and cinnamon stick. Saut√© for a minute until aromatic. Add diced mixed vegetables and potato to the pan. Stir and cook for 3-4 minutes until they are slightly tender.

3.

Pour the prepared korma paste into the pan with the vegetables. Mix well to combine. Add paneer cubes (if using) and stir gently.

4.

Reduce the heat to low and let the korma simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are cooked through and the flavors are well blended.

Garnish the veg korma with fresh cilantro leaves. Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
