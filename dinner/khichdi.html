Khichdi

Khichdi is a comforting and nutritious dish made with rice and lentils, typically seasoned with spices. Here's a basic recipe for making khichdi:

Ingridents:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup split yellow mung dal (moong dal)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 inch piece of ginger, grated or finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced (optional)
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped (optional)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (cilantro), chopped for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

1.

Rinse the rice and mung dal under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain and set aside.

2.

In a pressure cooker or a large pot, heat ghee or oil over medium heat.

3.

Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds. Let them splutter.

4.

Add grated ginger, minced garlic (if using), and chopped green chilies (if using). Sauté for a minute until fragrant.

1.

Add turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and red chili powder. Stir well to combine with the tempering.

2.

Add the rinsed rice and mung dal to the pot. Stir and sauté for 2-3 minutes to lightly toast the grains.

3.

Pour in 4 cups of water and season with salt according to taste.

4.

Bring the mixture to a boil.

5.

If using a pressure cooker, close the lid and cook on medium heat for 2 whistles. Then reduce the heat to low and cook for another 5-7 minutes.

6.

If using a pot, cover with a lid and cook on low heat until the rice and lentils are fully cooked and the mixture has a porridge-like consistency.

7.

Taste the chutney and adjust the seasoning if needed, adding more salt according to your preference.

Once the khichdi is cooked, fluff it gently with a fork. Serve hot, garnished with chopped coriander leaves if desired, and accompanied by lemon wedges for squeezing over the khichdi. Khichdi pairs well with yogurt, pickle, or any side dish of your choice.
