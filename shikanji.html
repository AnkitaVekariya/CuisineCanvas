Shikanji

Shikanji, also known as Nimbu Pani or Indian Lemonade, is a popular summer drink in India known for its refreshing and tangy flavor. Here's a simple recipe to make Shikanji at home:

Ingridents:

  • 4 cups cold water
  • 4-5 medium-sized lemons
  • 4-5 tablespoons sugar (adjust according to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • A pinch of ground black pepper
  • A few fresh mint leaves for garnish (optional)
  • Ice cubes (optional)

Instructions:

1.

Roll the lemons on a hard surface to soften them and extract more juice. Cut them in half and squeeze out the juice using a citrus juicer or your hands. Strain the juice to remove any seeds.

2.

In a large pitcher or jug, pour the cold water and add the freshly squeezed lemon juice. Stir in the sugar until it dissolves completely. Adjust the amount of sugar according to your taste preference.

3.

Add roasted cumin powder, black salt, and ground black pepper to the pitcher. These spices give Shikanji its distinct flavor. You can adjust the quantity of spices based on your taste preference.

4.

Stir the mixture thoroughly to ensure that the sugar and spices are evenly distributed. Chill:

5.

Refrigerate the Shikanji for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together and to chill the drink. Serve:

6.

Serve Shikanji chilled as a refreshing drink on hot summer days. It pairs well with spicy Indian snacks like chaat, pakoras, or samosas. You can also customize this recipe by adding a splash of soda water or sparkling water for fizziness or a few drops of rose water for added fragrance.
