Coconut Chutney

Certainly! Coconut chutney is a popular South Indian condiment that pairs well with dosa, idli, vada, or any other South Indian snack. Here's a simple recipe to make coconut chutney at home:

Ingridents:

  • 1 cup grated coconut (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 tablespoons roasted chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 2-3 green chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, peeled
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves (cilantro), chopped 1 tablespoon lemon juice or tamarind paste (optional, for tanginess) Salt, to taste Water, as needed For tempering:
  • 1 tablespoon oil (coconut oil or any vegetable oil)
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon urad dal (split black gram)
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 2-3 dried red chilies (broken into pieces)
  • Few curry leaves (optional)

Instructions:

1.

In a blender or food processor, add grated coconut, roasted chana dal, green chilies, peeled ginger, chopped coriander leaves, lemon juice or tamarind paste (if using), and salt.

2.

Add a little water to the blender to help with grinding. Start with about 1/4 cup of water and add more as needed to achieve the desired consistency.

3.

Blend all the ingredients together until you get a smooth and creamy chutney. You may need to scrape down the sides of the blender jar a few times to ensure even grinding.

4.

Once the chutney is smooth, transfer it to a serving bowl.

Prepare the tempering:

1.

Heat oil in a small pan over medium heat.

2.

Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.

3.

Add urad dal and fry until it turns golden brown.

4.

Add asafoetida, dried red chilies, and curry leaves (if using). Fry for a few seconds until the spices become fragrant.

5.

Pour the tempering over the coconut chutney in the serving bowl.

6.

Mix the tempering with the coconut chutney using a spoon.

7.

Taste the chutney and adjust the seasoning if needed, adding more salt or lemon juice according to your preference.

Your coconut chutney is now ready to serve! Enjoy it as a side dish with dosa, idli, vada, or any other South Indian snack.
