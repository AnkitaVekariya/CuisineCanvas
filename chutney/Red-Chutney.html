Red Chutney

Red chutney, also known as spicy tomato chutney or red coconut chutney, is a tangy and flavorful condiment commonly served with South Indian dishes like dosa, idli, and vada. Here's a simple recipe to make red chutney at home:

Ingridents:

  • 2 large tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • 2-3 dried red chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, peeled
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste or lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup grated coconut (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 tablespoon roasted peanuts or roasted chana dal (optional, for texture)
  • Few curry leaves
  • Salt, to taste
  • Water, as needed

Instructions:

1.

In a blender or food processor, add the roughly chopped tomatoes, dried red chilies, garlic, ginger, tamarind paste or lemon juice, grated coconut, roasted peanuts or chana dal (if using), and salt.

2.

Add a little water to the blender to help with grinding. Start with about 1/4 cup of water and add more as needed to achieve the desired consistency.

3.

Blend all the ingredients together until you get a smooth and creamy chutney. You may need to scrape down the sides of the blender jar a few times to ensure even grinding.

4.

Once the chutney is smooth, transfer it to a serving bowl.

Prepare the tempering:

1.

Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a small pan over medium heat.

2.

Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.

3.

Add cumin seeds and fry until they turn golden brown.

4.

Add asafoetida and curry leaves. Fry for a few seconds until the spices become fragrant.

5.

Pour the tempering over the raw mango chutney in the serving bowl.

6.

Mix the tempering with the chutney using a spoon.

7.

Taste the chutney and adjust the seasoning if needed, adding more salt according to your preference.

Your red chutney is now ready to serve! Enjoy it as a tangy accompaniment with rice, roti, dosa, idli, or any other Indian dish.
