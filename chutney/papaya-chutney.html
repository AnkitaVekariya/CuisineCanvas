Papaya Chutney

Papaya chutney is a sweet and tangy condiment that pairs well with various dishes, including Indian meals, grilled meats, and sandwiches. Here's a simple recipe to make papaya chutney at home:

Ingridents:

  • 2 cups raw papaya, peeled, seeded, and diced
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar or white vinegar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 2 teaspoon red chili flakes (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • Few curry leaves
  • Salt, to taste
  • Water, as needed

Instructions:

1.

Heat the oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and fennel seeds. Sauté for a minute or until the seeds start to splutter.

2.

Add diced papaya to the saucepan and sauté for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until slightly softened.

3.

Add brown sugar, vinegar, water, red chili flakes, ground ginger, ground cinnamon, ground cloves, and salt to the papaya in the saucepan. Stir well to combine.

4.

Bring the mixture to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer uncovered for about 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the papaya is cooked and the chutney thickens to your desired consistency.

Prepare the tempering:

1.

Taste the chutney and adjust the sweetness or spiciness by adding more sugar or red chili flakes if needed. Store any leftover papaya chutney in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week.

2.

Once the chutney is done, remove it from the heat and let it cool to room temperature.

3.

Transfer the papaya chutney to a serving bowl and garnish with chopped cilantro or mint leaves if desired.
