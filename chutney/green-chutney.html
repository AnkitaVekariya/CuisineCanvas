Green chutney

Green chutney, also known as coriander or mint chutney, is a versatile and flavorful condiment commonly used in Indian cuisine. It pairs well with various snacks like samosas, pakoras, sandwiches, and even as a spread for dosas and chaats. Here's a basic recipe for green chutney:

Ingridents:

  • 1 cup fresh coriander leaves (cilantro), chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, peeled and chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt, to taste Water, as needed

Instructions:

1.

Wash the coriander leaves and mint leaves thoroughly under running water to remove any dirt or impurities. Drain well.

2.

In a blender or food processor, add the chopped coriander leaves, mint leaves, green chilies, ginger, garlic, lemon juice, roasted peanuts or chana dal (if using), and salt.

3.

Add a little water to the blender to help with grinding. Start with about 1/4 cup of water and add more as needed to achieve the desired consistency.

4.

Blend all the ingredients together until you get a smooth and creamy chutney. You may need to scrape down the sides of the blender jar a few times to ensure even grinding.

5.

Once the chutney is smooth, transfer it to a serving bowl. Store any leftover green chutney in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days. Optionally, you can also freeze it in ice cube trays for longer storage.

Taste the chutney and adjust the seasoning if needed, adding more salt or lemon juice according to your preference.
