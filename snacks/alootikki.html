Aloo tikki

Aloo tikki is a popular Indian street food snack made with mashed potatoes and various spices. Here's a simple recipe to make aloo tikki at home:

Ingridents:

  • 4 medium-sized potatoes (boiled and mashed) 1 small onion (finely chopped) 1 green chili (finely chopped, adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 inch piece of ginger (grated) 2 tablespoons of fresh coriander leaves (chopped) 1 teaspoon of roasted cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon of chaat masala 1/2 teaspoon of red chili powder (adjust to taste) Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of cornflour or all-purpose flour (for binding) Oil for frying

Instructions:

1.

Prepare Potatoes: Boil the potatoes until they are completely cooked. Once cooked, peel and mash them well in a large mixing bowl.

2.

Add Spices: To the mashed potatoes, add chopped onions, green chili, grated ginger, chopped coriander leaves, roasted cumin powder, chaat masala, red chili powder, and salt. Mix everything well.

3.

Bind the Mixture: Add cornflour or all-purpose flour to the mixture. This helps in binding the mixture together. Mix well until you get a uniform dough-like consistency

4.

Shape the Tikki: Take a small portion of the mixture and shape it into a round flat patty or tikki. Repeat this process with the rest of the mixture

5.

Fry the Tikki: Heat oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully place the shaped tikkis into the pan. Fry them until they turn golden brown and crispy on both sides. Make sure to flip them occasionally for even cooking.

5.

Drain Excess Oil: Once the tikkis are fried, remove them from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

Serve: Serve the aloo tikkis hot with mint chutney, tamarind chutney, or ketchup. You can also serve them with yogurt and chaat masala for a tangy twist.
