Kachori

Kachori is a popular Indian snack made with a spicy filling enclosed in a crispy, deep-fried crust. Here's a recipe for making delicious kachoris:

Ingridents:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida) 4 tablespoons ghee or oil 1/2 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain) Salt to taste Water, as needed
  • For the filling: For the filling: 1 cup yellow moong dal (split yellow lentils), soaked for 4-5 hours 1 tablespoon oil 1 teaspoon cumin seeds 1/2 teaspoon asafoetida (hing) 1 teaspoon ginger paste 1 teaspoon green chili paste 1 teaspoon fennel seeds, crushed 1 teaspoon coriander powder 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder 1 teaspoon dry mango powder (amchur) Salt to taste Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

1.

Preparing the dough: In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, salt, and ghee/oil. Rub the ghee/oil into the flour until it resembles breadcrumbs. Gradually add water and knead into a stiff dough. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 20-30 minutes.

2.

Preparing the filling: Drain the soaked moong dal and grind it into a coarse paste without adding water. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and asafoetida. Let them splutter. Add ginger paste and green chili paste. Saut√© for a minute. Add the ground moong dal paste and cook on low to medium heat, stirring continuously, until it becomes thick and dry. Add crushed fennel seeds, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, dry mango powder, and salt. Mix well. Cook the mixture for another 2-3 minutes. Remove from heat and let the filling cool down.

3.

Assembling and frying: Divide the dough into equal-sized balls. Take one portion of the dough and roll it into a small disc of about 3-4 inches in diameter. Place a spoonful of the prepared filling in the center of the disc. Gather the edges of the disc to enclose the filling and pinch them together to seal. Gently flatten the filled dough ball with your palms. Repeat the process with the remaining dough and filling. Heat oil for deep frying in a kadai or deep frying pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, gently slide in the prepared kachoris, in batches, and fry until they turn golden brown and crispy from all sides.

Remove the fried kachoris using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on kitchen paper towels. Serve hot kachoris with tamarind chutney or mint chutney.
