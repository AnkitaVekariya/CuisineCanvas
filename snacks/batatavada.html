Batata Vada

Batata Vada is a popular Indian street food snack made with spiced mashed potatoes coated in gram flour batter and deep-fried until crispy and golden brown. It's often served with chutneys or as a filling in vada pav. Here's how to make Batata Vada at home:

Ingredients:

  • For the potato mixture:
    • 4 large potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed
    • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
    • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
    • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
    • 8-10 curry leaves, chopped
    • Salt, to taste
    • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • For the batter:
    • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
    • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
    • Salt, to taste
    • Water, as needed
  • Oil, for deep frying

Instructions:

1.

In a pan, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Let them splutter.

2.

Add minced garlic, grated ginger, chopped green chilies, and curry leaves to the pan. Saut√© for a minute.

3.

Add turmeric powder and asafoetida (hing) to the pan. Mix well.

4.

Add the mashed potatoes to the pan. Mix everything together, ensuring the spices are well incorporated into the potatoes. Cook for 2-3 minutes. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool slightly.

5.

Divide the potato mixture into equal-sized balls and shape them into smooth vadas (patties).

6.

In a separate bowl, prepare the batter by combining gram flour, turmeric powder, red chili powder, baking soda, salt, and water. Mix until smooth and lump-free, ensuring the batter is thick enough to coat the vadas.

7.

Heat oil for deep frying in a pan or kadhai over medium-high heat.

8.

Dip each potato vada into the prepared batter, ensuring it's evenly coated.

9.

Gently drop the coated vadas into the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crispy.

10.

Remove the fried batata vadas from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels.

Serve hot Batata Vadas with green chutney, tamarind chutney, or tomato ketchup. Enjoy!
