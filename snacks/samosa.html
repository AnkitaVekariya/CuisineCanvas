Samosa

Samosa is a popular Indian snack consisting of a crispy pastry shell filled with spicy potatoes, peas, and sometimes meat. It's a delicious treat enjoyed as a snack or appetizer. Here's how to make samosas at home:

Ingredients:

  • For the dough:
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1/4 cup vegetable oil or ghee
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • Water, as needed
  • For the filling:
    • 2 large potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed
    • 1/2 cup green peas, boiled or thawed if frozen
    • 1 small onion, finely chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
    • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
    • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil or ghee
    • Salt, to taste
    • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (optional)
  • Oil, for deep frying

Instructions:

1.

In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, salt, and vegetable oil or ghee for the dough. Mix well.

2.

Add water gradually and knead into a firm dough. Cover the dough and let it rest for 30 minutes.

3.

Meanwhile, prepare the filling. Heat vegetable oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat.

4.

Add cumin seeds to the hot oil and let them splutter. Then add chopped onions, minced garlic, grated ginger, and chopped green chilies. Saut√© until onions turn translucent.

5.

Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, and garam masala. Stir well and cook for a minute.

6.

Add mashed potatoes and boiled peas to the spice mixture. Mix everything together and cook for a few minutes. Season with salt and chopped fresh coriander leaves if using. Remove from heat and let the filling cool.

7.

Divide the dough into equal-sized balls. Roll out each ball into a thin oval-shaped sheet.

8.

Cut each rolled-out sheet in half to form two semi-circles. Take one semi-circle and fold it into a cone shape, sealing the edges with water.

9.

Fill each cone with the prepared potato-pea filling, then seal the open edge with water to form a triangular pocket.

10.

Heat oil for deep frying in a large pan or kadhai over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully slide in the samosas in batches and fry until golden brown and crispy.

11.

Remove the fried samosas from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels.

Serve hot samosas with mint chutney, tamarind chutney, or ketchup. Enjoy!
