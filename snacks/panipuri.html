PaniPuri

Panipuri, also known as golgappa or puchka in different regions of India, is a beloved street food snack consisting of small, hollow puris filled with a spicy and tangy water (pani) along with a filling of mashed potatoes, chickpeas, onions, and various chutneys. Here's a recipe to make panipuri at home:

Ingridents:

  • 1 cup semolina (sooji/rava)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • Water for kneading the dough
  • 1 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves
  • 2-3 green chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 1-inch piece of ginger
  • 1 small lemon (juiced)
  • 1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon chaat masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon black salt,1/2 teaspoon regular salt,1 tablespoon tamarind pulp

Instructions:

1.

Prepare Dough: In a mixing bowl, combine semolina, all-purpose flour, baking soda, and a pinch of salt. Gradually add water and knead into a stiff dough. Cover the dough and let it rest for about 30 minutes.

2.

Divide and Roll: After resting, knead the dough again briefly. Divide the dough into small portions and roll each portion into a small disc, around 2-3 inches in diameter.

3.

Fry Puris: Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Once the oil is hot, carefully slide in the rolled puris. Fry them until they puff up and turn golden brown. Remove them from the oil and place them on paper towels to drain excess oil. Let them cool completely.

4.

Blend Ingredients: In a blender, combine mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chilies, ginger, tamarind pulp, roasted cumin powder, chaat masala, black salt, regular salt, and lemon juice. Add water and blend until smooth.

1.

Strain: Strain the blended mixture using a fine mesh sieve into a large bowl. Add more water if needed to adjust the consistency. Refrigerate the pani until serving.

Fill and Serve: Fill each puri with a spoonful of the prepared filling mixture. Add a little sweet chutney and spicy green chutney according to your taste. Dip the filled puri into the chilled pani and serve immediately. Garnish: Optionally, top each panipuri with some sev for extra crunchiness.
