Papdi-chaat

Papdi Chaat is a popular Indian street food made with crispy fried dough wafers called papdi, topped with a combination of tangy chutneys, yogurt, and a variety of spices. Here's a recipe to make Papdi Chaat at home:

Ingridents:

  • For Papdi:
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tablespoons semolina (sooji)
  • 1/4 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Water, as needed
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

1.

Making Papdi: In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, semolina, carom seeds, salt, and oil. Gradually add water and knead into a stiff dough. Cover the dough and let it rest for about 15-20 minutes. Roll out the dough into a thin sheet and cut it into small rounds using a cookie cutter or lid. Prick each round with a fork to prevent puffing while frying. Heat oil for deep frying in a kadai or frying pan over medium heat. Fry the papdi in batches until they turn golden brown and crispy. Remove the fried papdi using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on kitchen paper towels.

2.

Assembling Papdi Chaat: Arrange the papdi on a serving plate. Top each papdi with boiled chickpeas (if using), diced boiled potato, chopped onion, and tomato. Drizzle sweet tamarind chutney and green coriander chutney over the papdi. Spoon beaten yogurt over the papdi. Sprinkle roasted cumin powder, chaat masala, red chili powder, and salt over the papdi.

Garnish with sev and chopped coriander leaves. Serve immediately with lemon wedges on the side.
