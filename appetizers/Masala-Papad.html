Masala Papad

Masala papad is a popular Indian appetizer made by topping a roasted or fried papad (a thin, crisp Indian flatbread made from lentil or chickpea flour) with a spicy and tangy mixture of chopped vegetables and spices. Here's a simple recipe to make masala papad:

Ingridents:

  • 4 papads (any variety such as moong dal papad or urad dal papad)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 small tomato, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cucumber, finely chopped
  • 1 small green chili, finely chopped (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon chaat masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Lemon juice, to taste
  • Sev (thin gram flour noodles) for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

1.

Prepare the Papads: Roast or fry the papads according to the package instructions until they are crisp. Set aside.

2.

Prepare the Masala Topping: In a mixing bowl, combine the chopped onion, tomato, cucumber, green chili (if using), and coriander leaves. Add chaat masala, roasted cumin powder, red chili powder, salt, and lemon juice to the chopped vegetables. Mix well to combine. Adjust the seasoning according to your taste.

3.

Assemble the Masala Papad: Place the roasted or fried papads on serving plates. Evenly spread the prepared masala topping over each papad. If desired, sprinkle some sev on top of the masala topping for extra crunch and flavor.

Serve the masala papad immediately as an appetizer or starter before the main course. Enjoy the crispy texture of the papad combined with the spicy and tangy flavors of the masala topping.
