Hummus

Hummus is a creamy dip made from chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, and garlic, blended together until smooth. It's a popular Middle Eastern dish and is often served as an appetizer or snack. Here's how to make hummus at home:

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (15 ounces) chickpeas (garbanzo beans), drained and rinsed
  • 3 tablespoons tahini (sesame seed paste)
  • 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil, plus more for serving
  • 1-2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Juice of 1 lemon (about 3 tablespoons)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2-3 tablespoons water, or as needed
  • Paprika, for garnish
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish
  • Optional toppings: olive oil, pine nuts, olives, chopped tomatoes, or parsley

Instructions:

1.

In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, tahini, olive oil, minced garlic, lemon juice, ground cumin, and a pinch of salt.

2.

Blend the mixture until smooth, scraping down the sides of the bowl as needed. If the hummus is too thick, add water, 1 tablespoon at a time, until desired consistency is reached.

3.

Taste and adjust seasoning, adding more salt or lemon juice if needed.

4.

Transfer the hummus to a serving bowl. Create a shallow well in the center with the back of a spoon and drizzle with extra virgin olive oil.

5.

Garnish with a sprinkle of paprika and chopped parsley. Add any optional toppings if desired.

Serve hummus with pita bread, crackers, or vegetable sticks. Enjoy!
