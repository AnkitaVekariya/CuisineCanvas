Schezwan Chutney

Schezwan chutney, also spelled as Szechuan chutney, is a spicy and flavorful condiment originating from Chinese cuisine. It's commonly used in Indo-Chinese dishes like Schezwan fried rice, noodles, and appetizers. Here's a simple recipe to make Schezwan chutney at home:

Ingridents:

  • 15-20 dried red chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 5-6 garlic cloves
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, peeled
  • 2 tablespoons oil (sesame oil or any vegetable oil)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar or jaggery
  • Salt, to taste
  • Water, as needed

Instructions:

1.

In a blender or food processor, add the soaked red chilies, garlic cloves, and peeled ginger. Blend until you get a smooth paste. You may need to add a little water to facilitate blending.

2.

Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the red chili-garlic-ginger paste.

3.

Saut√© the paste for a few minutes until it becomes fragrant and the raw smell of garlic and ginger disappears.

4.

Add tomato ketchup, soy sauce, vinegar, brown sugar or jaggery, and salt to the pan. Mix well to combine all the ingredients. Cook the mixture over low heat for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the oil separates and the chutney thickens to your desired consistency

Prepare the tempering:

1.

Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a small pan over medium heat.

2.

Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.

3.

Add cumin seeds and fry until they turn golden brown.

4.

Add asafoetida and curry leaves. Fry for a few seconds until the spices become fragrant.

5.

Pour the tempering over the raw mango chutney in the serving bowl.

6.

Mix the tempering with the chutney using a spoon.

7.

Taste the chutney and adjust the seasoning if needed, adding more salt according to your preference.

Your raw mango chutney is now ready to serve! Enjoy it as a tangy accompaniment with rice, roti, dosa, idli, or any other Indian dish.
