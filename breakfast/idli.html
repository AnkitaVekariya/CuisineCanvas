idli

Idli is a traditional South Indian breakfast dish made from fermented rice and urad dal batter. These soft, fluffy, and savory cakes are usually served with chutney and sambar. Here's a basic recipe to make idli at home:

Ingridents:

  • 2 cups idli rice (parboiled rice)
  • 1 cup whole urad dal (split black gram)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water, as needed for soaking and grinding

Instructions:

1.

Wash the idli rice and urad dal separately under running water until the water runs clear. Soak them in water separately for at least 4-6 hours or overnight. Grinding:

2.

After soaking, drain the water from the urad dal and grind it in a wet grinder or blender until smooth and fluffy. You may need to add water gradually while grinding to achieve a smooth consistency. The batter should be thick and fluffy.

3.

Transfer the ground urad dal batter to a large mixing bowl.

4.

Next, drain the water from the soaked idli rice and grind it to a slightly coarse texture, similar to semolina. Add water as needed while grinding.

5.

Transfer the ground idli rice batter to the same mixing bowl with the urad dal batter.

6.

Add salt to the batter and mix well using your hands. The batter should have a thick pouring consistency.
