upma

Here's a simple and delicious recipe for Upma, a popular South Indian breakfast dish made with semolina (sooji), vegetables, and aromatic spices:

Ingridents:

  • 1 cup semolina (sooji/rava)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 inch ginger, finely chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, finely chopped
  • 2-3 tablespoons chopped mixed vegetables (optional)
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste
  • A handful of chopped cilantro leaves for garnish (optional) Lemon wedges for serving (optional)

Instructions:

1.

Dry roast the semolina in a pan over medium heat until it turns light golden brown. Keep stirring continuously to prevent burning. Once roasted, transfer it to a plate and set aside. Cooking Upma:

2.

Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add cumin seeds and sauté for a few seconds until they start to crackle.

3.

Add chopped ginger and green chilies. Sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears. Add chopped onions and cook until they turn translucent.

4.

Add chopped vegetables (carrot, peas, and any other vegetables you're using). Sauté for a couple of minutes until they are slightly tender. Adding Semolina:

5.

Pour water into the pan and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and slowly add the roasted semolina to the boiling water while stirring continuously to avoid lumps. Keep stirring and cook for a few minutes until the semolina absorbs all the water and becomes soft and fluffy. Seasoning:

6.

Add salt to taste and mix well. Adjust the consistency by adding more water if necessary. Cover the pan with a lid and let the upma simmer for 2-3 minutes on low heat to allow the flavors to meld together. Garnish and Serve:

6.

Garnish the upma with chopped cilantro leaves (optional). Serve hot with a side of lemon wedges for a tangy flavor. Upma pairs well with coconut chutney, pickle, or yogurt.
