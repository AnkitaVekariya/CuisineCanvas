khaman

Khaman is a popular Gujarati snack made from gram flour (besan) and typically served as a breakfast or snack. It's known for its fluffy texture and tangy flavor. Here's a recipe to make Khaman at home:

Ingridents:

  • cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1/4 cup semolina (sooji/rava)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-green chili paste
  • 1/2 cup yogurt (plain curd)
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 1 teaspoon fruit salt (eno) or 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • -3 green chilies, slit lengthwise A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves Grated coconut for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

1.

In a large mixing bowl, combine gram flour, semolina, ginger-green chili paste, yogurt, water, lemon juice, sugar, and salt. Mix well to form a smooth batter without any lumps. The consistency should be thick yet pourable. Let the batter rest for about 15-20 minutes. Prepare the steamer:

2.

Grease a shallow dish or thali with oil and keep it ready. Also, prepare your steamer by bringing water to a boil. Add fruit salt:

3.

Just before steaming, add fruit salt (eno) or baking soda to the batter. Mix gently and immediately pour the batter into the greased dish. Steam the khaman:

4.

Place the greased dish into the steamer and cover it with a lid. Steam the khaman for about 15-20 minutes on medium heat or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Once done, turn off the heat and remove the dish from the steamer. Allow it to cool for a few minutes. Prepare the tempering:

5.

Heat oil in a small pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add sesame seeds, slit green chilies, and curry leaves. Saute for a few seconds. Add water and bring it to a boil. Turn off the heat. Pour the tempering:

6.

Serve the khaman warm with green chutney or tamarind chutney. It can also be served with a sprinkle of sev or nylon sev for extra crunchiness.
