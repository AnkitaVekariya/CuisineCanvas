khandvi

Khandvi is a popular Gujarati snack made from gram flour (besan). It has a soft, melt-in-your-mouth texture with a tangy flavor. Here's a simple recipe to make khandvi at home:

Ingridents:

  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1 cup yogurt (curd), whisked
  • 2 cups water 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon green chili paste 1 tablespoon oil
  • 2 tablespoons oil 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves 2 tablespoons grated coconut (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro (coriander) leaves for garnish

Instructions:

1.

In a mixing bowl, combine gram flour, whisked yogurt, water, turmeric powder, salt, ginger paste, and green chili paste. Mix well to form a smooth batter without any lumps. Cook the Batter:

2.

Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a non-stick pan or a heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat. Pour the batter into the pan and continuously stir to prevent lumps from forming. Cook the batter until it thickens and becomes smooth. It should take about 8-10 minutes. Keep stirring continuously to avoid any lumps.

3.

Once the batter is cooked, immediately pour it onto a clean, greased surface like a stainless steel plate or a marble countertop. Quickly spread it into a thin, even layer using a spatula. Cut and Roll:

4.

After spreading the batter, let it cool for a minute or two. Then, using a knife, cut the spread batter into thin strips. Gently roll each strip tightly to form a roll. Repeat this process with all the strips.

5.

Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add sesame seeds, asafoetida, slit green chilies, and curry leaves. Saute for a few seconds until fragrant. Temper the Khandvi Rolls:

6.

Pour the tempering mixture over the rolled khandvi rolls evenly. Garnish:

7.

Allow the khandvi to cool down for a few minutes before serving. Serve it with green chutney or tamarind chutney for extra flavor.
