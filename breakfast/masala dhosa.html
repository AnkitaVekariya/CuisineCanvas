Masala Dosa

Masala Dosa is a popular South Indian dish consisting of a crispy dosa (fermented rice and lentil crepe) filled with a spicy potato filling. It's typically served with chutney and sambar. Here's how you can make Masala Dosa at home:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice (any variety)
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (black gram lentils)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee for cooking
  • 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • A few curry leaves
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil

Instructions:

1.

Rinse the rice and urad dal separately under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak them in separate bowls with enough water for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight. If using fenugreek seeds, add them to the urad dal while soaking.

2.

After soaking, drain the water from both the rice and urad dal. Transfer them to a blender or grinder. Add a little water and grind to a smooth batter. The consistency should be similar to pancake batter. Add salt to taste and mix well.

3.

Transfer the batter to a large bowl and cover it with a clean cloth. Allow it to ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours, or until it increases in volume and becomes slightly airy. Once the batter is fermented, gently stir it. If it's too thick, add a little water to adjust the consistency.

4.

To prepare the potato filling, heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. When they start to splutter, add the finely chopped onions, green chilies, and grated ginger. Saut√© until the onions turn golden brown.

5.

Add turmeric powder, curry leaves, and the mashed potatoes. Mix well and cook for a few minutes. Add salt to taste. Finally, add fresh coriander leaves and mix. Remove from heat and set aside.

6.

Heat a non-stick skillet or dosa pan over medium heat. Once hot, pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the pan. Using the back of the ladle, spread the batter in a circular motion to form a thin layer. Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa.

7.

Cook the dosa for 1-2 minutes, or until the edges start to lift and the bottom turns golden brown. Place a spoonful of the prepared potato filling in the center of the dosa. Fold the dosa over the filling.

8.

Remove the dosa from the pan and serve hot with chutney, sambar, or any desired accompaniment. Repeat the process with the remaining batter and filling, greasing the pan lightly with oil or ghee before making each dosa.
