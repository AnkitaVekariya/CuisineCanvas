Rajma

Rajma, also known as kidney beans curry, is a popular North Indian dish that's both nutritious and delicious. Here's a traditional recipe for

Ingridents:

  • 1 cup dried rajma (kidney beans)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste Water, as needed

Instructions:

Preparing Rajma:

Rinse the dried rajma under running water and soak them overnight or for at least 8 hours. Drain the soaking water and rinse the rajma again before cooking. If using a pressure cooker, add soaked rajma with fresh water (enough to cover the beans by about an inch). Pressure cook for about 15-20 minutes until the beans are soft and tender. If cooking in a pot, it may take longer, approximately 1 to 1.5 hours.

Cooking the Masala:

Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw aroma disappears. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy and oil starts separating from the masala.

Spices:

Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and red chili powder. Stir well and cook the masala for a couple of minutes.

Combining Rajma and Masala:

Add the cooked rajma along with its cooking water to the masala. Mix well and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes on low heat, allowing the flavors to blend together. Add more water if necessary to achieve the desired consistency.

Final Touch:

Check for salt and adjust if necessary. Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves.

Serve hot with steamed rice or roti. Rajma also pairs well with naan or paratha
