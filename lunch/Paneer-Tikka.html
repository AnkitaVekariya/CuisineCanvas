Paneer Tikka

Paneer tikka is a popular Indian appetizer made with marinated paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cubes, skewered, and grilled or baked to perfection. Here's a simple recipe to make paneer tikka at home:

Ingridents:

  • 250 grams paneer, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup thick yogurt (hung curd) 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste 1 tablespoon oil 1 onion, cut into chunks (optional, for skewering)
  • 1 bell pepper (capsicum), cut into chunks (optional, for skewering) Few cherry tomatoes (optional, for skewering) Cooking oil or ghee, for grilling or baking
  • Chaat masala for sprinkling (optional)
  • Lemon wedges for serving Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

Marinating Paneer:

In a mixing bowl, combine thick yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, red chili powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder, garam masala, salt, and oil. Mix well to form a smooth marinade. Add paneer cubes to the marinade and gently coat them with the mixture. Allow the paneer to marinate for at least 1-2 hours in the refrigerator. The longer it marinates, the more flavorful it will be.

Preparing Skewers (Optional):

If using skewers, soak them in water for at least 30 minutes to prevent them from burning during grilling. Thread the marinated paneer cubes, onion chunks, bell pepper chunks, and cherry tomatoes alternately onto the skewers. Repeat until all the skewers are filled.

Grilling or Baking:

Preheat your grill or oven to medium-high heat. Brush the grill grates or baking tray with oil to prevent sticking. Place the skewers on the grill or baking tray, leaving some space between each skewer. Grill or bake the paneer tikka for 10-12 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the paneer is golden brown and slightly charred on the edges. If using an oven, you can also broil the paneer tikka for a few minutes at the end for extra charred flavor.

Finishing Touch:

Once cooked, remove the paneer tikka from the grill or oven and transfer them to a serving plate. Sprinkle some chaat masala on top for extra flavor (optional). Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with lemon wedges on the side.

Serve paneer tikka as a starter or appetizer with mint chutney, tamarind chutney, or yogurt dip on the side. You can also serve it as a side dish with naan, roti, or rice.
