Pan Bhaji

Pav Bhaji is a popular Indian street food dish consisting of a spicy and flavorful vegetable curry (bhaji) served with buttered and toasted bread rolls (pav). Here's a recipe to make Pav Bhaji at home:

Ingridents:

  • For the bhaji (vegetable curry):
  • 4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed 1 cup cauliflower florets, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup green peas (fresh or frozen) 1/2 cup carrots, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup bell peppers (capsicum), finely chopped 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, finely chopped 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon pav bhaji masala 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder Salt, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons butter 2 tablespoons oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped, for garnishing
  • Salt, to taste
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions:

1.

Heat oil and butter together in a large pan or kadai over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add chopped onions and green chilies. Saute until the onions turn translucent.

2.

Add ginger-garlic paste and saut√© for a minute until the raw smell disappears. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy. Add pav bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes.

3.

Add mashed potatoes, chopped cauliflower, green peas, carrots, and bell peppers. Mix everything well to combine. Mash the vegetables with a potato masher or the back of a spoon. Keep mashing until you get a smooth and uniform consistency. You can leave some small chunks for texture if desired. Add a little water if the mixture is too dry. Cook the bhaji for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are cooked through and the flavors are well blended.

4.

Once the bhaji is cooked, garnish with chopped coriander leaves and a dollop of butter. Mix well. Meanwhile, heat a griddle or tawa over medium heat. Apply some butter on the sliced pav and toast them on the griddle until they turn golden brown and crispy on both sides.

Serve the hot and spicy bhaji with toasted pav, lemon wedges, and some chopped onions on the side.
