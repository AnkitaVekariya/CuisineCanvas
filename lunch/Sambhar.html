Sambhar

Sambar is a popular South Indian dish made with lentils, vegetables, and a flavorful spice blend. It's typically served with rice, idli, dosa, or vada. Here's a simple recipe to make sambar at home:

Ingridents:

  • For sambar: 1/2 cup toor dal (pigeon peas) 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 small tomato, chopped 1 small carrot, diced
  • 1 small potato, diced 1 small eggplant (brinjal), diced
  • 1/2 cup chopped mixed vegetables (beans, pumpkin, drumsticks, etc.) 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon sambar powder (store-bought or homemade) 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder Salt, to taste
  • For tempering (tadka):
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2-3 dried red chilies
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Few curry leaves

Instructions:

1.

Rinse the toor dal under running water until the water runs clear. Then, soak the dal in water for about 30 minutes. In a pressure cooker, add the soaked toor dal along with chopped onion, tomato, diced carrot, potato, eggplant, mixed vegetables, tamarind paste, sambar powder, turmeric powder, and salt.

2.

Add about 3 cups of water to the pressure cooker and mix well. Close the pressure cooker with its lid and cook the dal and vegetables over medium heat for about 4-5 whistles, or until the dal and vegetables are cooked and soft.

3.

Once the pressure releases naturally, open the pressure cooker and mash the dal and vegetables lightly using a spoon or a potato masher. Adjust the consistency by adding more water if needed. Place the pressure cooker back on the stove over low heat. Prepare the tempering (tadka) by heating oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Then, add cumin seeds, dried red chilies, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Fry for a few seconds until the spices become fragrant.

4.

Pour the tempering over the cooked sambar in the pressure cooker. Mix well. Let the sambar simmer for another 5-10 minutes over low heat, allowing the flavors to blend. Garnish the sambar with chopped coriander leaves.

Serve hot sambar with steamed rice, idli, dosa, or vada.
