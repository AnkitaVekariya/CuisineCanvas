Aloo Paratha

Aloo Paratha is a delicious Indian flatbread stuffed with spiced mashed potatoes. Here's a simple recipe to make Aloo Paratha:

Ingridents:

  • For the dough: 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta) Water, as needed Salt, to taste
  • For the potato filling:
  • 3-4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped (optional)
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon amchur powder (dry mango powder) or chaat masala
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander leaves Salt, to taste
  • Oil or ghee, for cooking

Instructions:

Prepare the dough:

In a large mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour and salt. Gradually add water and knead to form a soft and smooth dough. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 15-20 minutes.

Prepare the potato filling:

In another bowl, combine the mashed potatoes, finely chopped onion (if using), green chilies, grated ginger, cumin seeds, coriander powder, garam masala, amchur powder or chaat masala, chopped coriander leaves, and salt. Mix everything well to combine.

Divide the dough and filling:

After resting, divide the dough into equal-sized balls, slightly smaller than the size of a tennis ball. Divide the potato filling into portions accordingly.

Roll out the dough balls:

Take one portion of dough and flatten it into a small disk using your fingers. Dust it lightly with flour and roll it out into a small circle, about 4-5 inches in diameter.

Stuff and seal the paratha:

Place a portion of the potato filling in the center of the rolled-out dough circle. Bring the edges of the dough together to cover the filling and pinch to seal, ensuring there are no cracks.

Roll out the stuffed dough ball:

Gently flatten the stuffed dough ball with your hands. Dust it lightly with flour and roll it out into a circle, about 6-7 inches in diameter. Be careful not to put too much pressure as you roll, as this may cause the filling to burst out.

Cook the paratha:

Heat a tawa (griddle) or a non-stick pan over medium heat. Once hot, place the rolled-out paratha on the tawa. Cook for about 1-2 minutes on one side until small bubbles start to appear on the surface. Flip the paratha and drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges and on the surface. Cook for another 1-2 minutes, pressing gently with a spatula, until both sides are golden brown and crispy.

Serve the hot and crispy Aloo Parathas with fresh yogurt, pickle, or any chutney of your choice.
