Veg.Biryani

Here's a delicious and aromatic recipe for Veg Biryani, a flavorful Indian rice dish cooked with assorted vegetables and fragrant spices:

Ingridents:

  • For Rice: 2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes and drained
  • 4 cups water 1 bay leaf
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods 4-5 whole cloves
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick Salt to taste
  • For Vegetable Mixture:
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped 1 cup mixed vegetables (such as carrots, peas, green beans, cauliflower), diced
  • 1 cup yogurt 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Whole Spices:
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • -5 whole cloves/span>
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick

Instructions:

Cooking Rice:

In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add the soaked and drained rice along with whole spices (bay leaf, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon) and salt. Cook the rice until it's about 70-80% done. Drain the rice and set aside.

Preparing Vegetable Mixture:

Heat ghee or oil in a separate pan. Add sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy. Add mixed vegetables and sauté for a few minutes until they are partially cooked. In a bowl, whisk yogurt with red chili powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Add this mixture to the pan and mix well. Cook for a couple of minutes until the vegetables are coated with the spices.

Layering Biryani:

In a heavy-bottomed pan or biryani pot, spread a layer of half-cooked rice at the bottom. Spread a layer of the cooked vegetable mixture on top of the rice. Repeat the layers with the remaining rice and vegetable mixture. Sprinkle some fried onions and chopped cilantro leaves on the top.

Dum Cooking:

Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. You can seal the edges with dough to trap the steam (optional). Cook on low heat (dum) for about 15-20 minutes until the rice is fully cooked and aromatic flavors are infused.

Once done, gently fluff the biryani with a fork. Serve hot with raita (yogurt dip) or your favorite side dish.
