Dal Tadka

Dal Tadka is a popular Indian lentil dish characterized by its rich, flavorful tempering of spices. Here's a basic recipe for making Dal Tadka:

Ingridents:

  • For Dal:
  • 1 cup yellow split pigeon peas (toor dal) 3 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder Salt to taste
  • For Tadka (Tempering):
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala

Instructions:

1.

Rinse the toor dal under running water until the water runs clear. Drain the water and transfer the dal to a pressure cooker.

2.

Add 3 cups of water, turmeric powder, and salt to the pressure cooker with the dal. Close the lid and pressure cook for about 3-4 whistles, or until the dal is soft and cooked through.

3.

Once the pressure releases naturally, open the pressure cooker and mash the dal using the back of a spoon or a potato masher. Set aside. In a separate pan, heat ghee or oil over medium heat for tempering. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds to the hot ghee/oil. Let them splutter. Add minced garlic, ginger, and slit green chilies. Saut√© until fragrant.

4.

Add finely chopped onions and cook until they turn golden brown. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy. Add red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes. Pour the cooked dal into the pan with the tempering. Mix everything well and let it simmer for a few minutes, allowing the flavors to blend together. Adjust the consistency of the dal by adding water if it's too thick. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

Serve hot with rice or roti.
