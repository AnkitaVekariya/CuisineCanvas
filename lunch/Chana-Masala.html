Chana Masala

Chana Masala, also known as chole masala, is a popular Indian dish made with chickpeas (chana) cooked in a spicy and tangy tomato-based sauce. Here's a basic recipe to make it at home:

Ingridents:

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas (or 3 cans of cooked chickpeas)
  • 2 medium-sized onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Instructions:

Preparing Chickpeas:

If using dried chickpeas, soak them overnight in water. Then, boil them with a pinch of salt until they are soft. If using canned chickpeas, drain and rinse them under cold water.

Cooking the Masala:

Heat oil or ghee in a large pan or pressure cooker. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw aroma disappears. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy and oil starts separating from the masala.

Spices:

Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala, and red chili powder. Stir well and cook the masala for a couple of minutes.

Adding Chickpeas:

Once the chutney is smooth, transfer it to a serving bowl.

Cooking:

Add water to adjust the consistency of the gravy. Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to blend together.

Final Touch:

Check for salt and adjust if necessary. Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves.

Serve hot with rice, naan, or roti. Squeeze some lemon juice on top before serving for an extra tangy flavor.
