Thandai

Thandai is a traditional Indian drink made with a blend of nuts, spices, and milk. It's especially popular during festivals like Holi and Mahashivratri. Here's a simple recipe to make thandai at home:

Ingridents:

  • 1/4 cup almonds
  • 1/4 cup cashews
  • 2 tablespoons melon seeds (magaz)
  • 2 tablespoons poppy seeds (khus khus)
  • 1 tablespoon fennel seeds (saunf)
  • 1 tablespoon whole black peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon whole green cardamom pods
  • 1 teaspoon rose petals (dried or fresh)s
  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • Saffron strands (optional, for garnish)
  • Chopped pistachios or almonds (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

1.

In a dry skillet or pan, lightly roast the almonds, cashews, melon seeds, poppy seeds, fennel seeds, black peppercorns, and cardamom pods over low heat for a few minutes until they become fragrant. Be careful not to burn them. Remove from heat and let them cool.

2.

Once cooled, transfer the roasted nuts and spices to a blender or food processor. Add the rose petals.

3.

Grind the mixture into a fine powder. You may need to grind it in batches to ensure everything is ground evenly.

4.

In a large bowl, combine the ground nut and spice mixture with the whole milk and sugar. Stir well until the sugar is dissolved.

5.

Cover the bowl and refrigerate the thandai mixture for at least 2-3 hours or overnight to allow the flavors to meld.

6.

Before serving, strain the thandai mixture through a fine mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove any coarse particles. Press down on the solids to extract all the flavors.

7.

Discard the solids and transfer the strained thandai mixture to a serving pitcher.

Serve the chilled thandai in glasses, garnished with a few saffron strands and chopped pistachios or almonds, if desired. Enjoy your homemade thandai as a refreshing and flavorful drink, especially during festive occasions!
