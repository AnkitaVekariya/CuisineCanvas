Faluda

Falooda is a delicious and refreshing dessert beverage popular in South Asia. It's made with a combination of vermicelli noodles, basil seeds (sabja), milk, ice cream, and flavored syrups. Here's a traditional recipe for making falooda at home:

Ingridents:

  • 1/4 cup falooda sev (vermicelli noodles)
  • 2 tablespoons basil seeds (sabja/tukmaria)
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 2 tablespoons sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon rose water
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A few drops of pink or green food color (optional)
  • 2 scoops of vanilla or rose ice cream
  • 1/4 cup mixed dry fruits (almonds, cashews, pistachios), chopped (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon Rooh Afza or rose syrup
  • Chopped fresh fruits like strawberries or mangoes (optional)
  • Whipped cream for garnish (optional)
  • Cherries or tutti-frutti for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

1.

Prepare the Falooda Sev: Cook the falooda sev according to the package instructions. Usually, it involves boiling the sev in water until it's cooked but still retains a bit of bite. Once cooked, drain and rinse the sev under cold water to stop the cooking process. Set aside to cool.

2.

Soak the Basil Seeds: Rinse the basil seeds in water and soak them in about 1/2 cup of water. Let them soak for about 15-20 minutes until they swell up and form a gel-like consistency.

3.

Prepare the Milk Mixture: In a saucepan, heat the whole milk over medium heat. Add sugar, cardamom powder, and rose water to the milk. Stir well until the sugar is completely dissolved. Optionally, you can add a few drops of food color to the milk for a vibrant hue.

4.

Assemble the Falooda: In serving glasses or bowls, start by adding a layer of cooked falooda sev at the bottom. Add a spoonful of soaked basil seeds over the sev. Pour the sweetened milk mixture over the sev and basil seeds, filling the glass about halfway.

5.

Add Ice Cream and Syrup: Place a scoop of vanilla or rose ice cream on top of the milk mixture in each glass. Drizzle Rooh Afza or rose syrup over the ice cream.

6.

Add Toppings: Garnish the falooda with chopped dry fruits and fresh fruits if using. Optionally, top with a dollop of whipped cream and a cherry or tutti-frutti for decoration.

Serve the falooda immediately with a long spoon and a straw for enjoying all the layers of flavors and textures.
