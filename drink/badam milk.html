Badam Milk

Badam milk, also known as almond milk, is a delicious and nutritious beverage. Here's a simple recipe to make badam milk at home:

Ingridents:

  • 1 cup almonds (badam)
  • 4 cups milk (you can use dairy milk or any plant-based milk like almond milk for a vegan version)
  • 4-5 tablespoons sugar (adjust according to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom (elaichi) powder
  • A pinch of saffron strands (kesar), soaked in a tablespoon of warm milk (optional)
  • A few chopped pistachios for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

1.

Soak the almonds in warm water for 4-6 hours or overnight. This will soften the almonds and make them easier to blend.

2.

After soaking, drain the water from the almonds and rinse them with fresh water. Peel the skins off the almonds. The skins should easily slide off after soaking.

3.

In a blender, add the peeled almonds and a little bit of milk (about 1/2 cup) to facilitate blending. Blend until you get a smooth paste. You may need to scrape down the sides of the blender a few times to ensure all the almonds are blended.

4.

In a saucepan, heat the remaining milk over medium heat. Add the almond paste to the milk and mix well. Cook the mixture for 5-7 minutes, stirring frequently, until it thickens slightly.

5.

Add sugar according to your taste preference and ground cardamom powder to the almond milk mixture. If using saffron, add the soaked saffron strands along with the milk they were soaked in for added flavor and color. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

6.

Enjoy badam milk as a nutritious and satisfying beverage on its own or paired with your favorite snacks. You can also serve it as a dessert alongside Indian sweets like jalebi or gulab jamun. Adjust the sweetness and flavorings according to your taste preference for a personalized touch.
