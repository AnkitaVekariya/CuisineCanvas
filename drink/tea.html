Tea

It seems you're referring to "cha," which is a traditional Tibetan butter tea. Here's a basic recipe to make cha:

Ingridents:

  • 3 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons loose black tea leaves (or 2 black tea bags)
  • 1/2 cup milk (traditionally yak milk is used, but you can use any milk of your choice)
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter (traditionally yak butter is used, but you can use any unsalted butter)
  • Salt, to taste (optional)

Instructions:

1.

In a saucepan, bring the water to a boil.

2.

Once the water is boiling, add the loose black tea leaves or black tea bags to the saucepan. Let it simmer for about 5 minutes to brew the tea.

3.

After 5 minutes, reduce the heat to low and add the milk and unsalted butter to the saucepan.

4.

Allow the mixture to simmer for another 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the butter has melted and the flavors have infused.

5.

If desired, add a pinch of salt to taste. Traditionally, a small amount of salt is added to enhance the flavor of the tea.

6.

Once the tea is ready, remove it from the heat and strain it into cups or a teapot to remove the tea leaves or tea bags. Serve the cha hot and enjoy its rich and buttery flavor.
