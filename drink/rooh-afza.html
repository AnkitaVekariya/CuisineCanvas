Rooh Afza

It Rooh Afza is a popular sweet and tangy syrup used to make refreshing drinks. Here's a basic recipe to make Rooh Afza syrup at home:

Ingridents:

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water 1 tablespoon dried rose petals (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon rose water
  • 1 teaspoon kewra water (optional)
  • Â½ teaspoon citric acid
  • A pinch of edible red food color (optional)

Instructions:

1.

In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Heat over medium heat, stirring continuously until the sugar completely dissolves. Let the mixture simmer for 5-7 minutes until it slightly thickens to a syrupy consistency.

2.

Once the syrup is ready, add dried rose petals (if using) and continue to simmer for another 5 minutes to infuse the flavor. Then, add fresh lemon juice, rose water, kewra water (if using), and citric acid. Stir well to combine.

3.

If you prefer, add a pinch of edible red food color to give the syrup a vibrant pink hue. Adjust the amount according to your preference. Cool and Strain:

4.

Allow the syrup to cool down to room temperature. Once cooled, strain the syrup through a fine mesh sieve to remove any solids or impurities.

5.

Transfer the strained syrup into clean, sterilized bottles or jars. Store the Rooh Afza syrup in the refrigerator. It can last for several weeks when stored properly.

6.

To serve, mix 1 part Rooh Afza syrup with 5-6 parts cold water or milk. Adjust the ratio according to your taste preference. You can also add ice cubes and garnish with fresh mint leaves, sliced fruits like cucumber or lemon, and basil seeds (tukmaria) for added flavor and texture. Enjoy your homemade Rooh Afza drink! Adjust the sweetness and flavorings according to your taste preferences for a perfect summer cooler.
