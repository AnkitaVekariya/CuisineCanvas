Murukku

Murukku is a popular South Indian snack made with rice flour and flavored with spices. It has a crunchy texture and is often enjoyed with tea or coffee. Here's how to make murukku at home:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups rice flour
  • 1/2 cup urad dal flour
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter or ghee
  • Salt, to taste
  • Oil, for deep frying
  • Water, as needed

Instructions:

1.

In a large mixing bowl, combine rice flour, urad dal flour, cumin seeds, sesame seeds, asafoetida, red chili powder, melted butter or ghee, and salt.

2.

Mix everything well to combine. Gradually add water, a little at a time, and knead into a smooth and stiff dough.

3.

Heat oil for deep frying in a heavy-bottomed pan or kadhai over medium heat.

4.

While the oil is heating, prepare the murukku press. Grease the murukku press with a little oil and place a small portion of the dough inside the press.

5.

Press the dough out of the murukku press directly into the hot oil in a circular motion, forming small spiral-shaped murukkus.

6.

Fry the murukkus in batches until they turn golden brown and crispy. Use a slotted spoon to remove them from the oil and drain on paper towels.

7.

Repeat the process with the remaining dough, frying the murukkus until all the dough is used up.

Once cooled, store the murukkus in an airtight container. Enjoy these crunchy snacks with tea or coffee!
